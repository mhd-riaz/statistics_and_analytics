## Overview

**Inferential Statistics** uses sample data to draw conclusions about a larger population. While Unit 1 (Descriptive Statistics) focused on _summarising_ data, this unit focuses on _generalising_ from samples to populations and _testing_ whether observed patterns are real or just due to chance.

The unit progresses through five blocks:

1. **Standardization** — Converting raw values into comparable units (Z-scores), understanding the difference between standard deviation and standard error, and computing cumulative probabilities.
2. **Sampling** — How sample means behave, what a sampling distribution looks like, and why the Central Limit Theorem lets us use normal-curve methods on almost any dataset.
3. **Estimation & Confidence** — Moving from a single-number guess (point estimate) to a range (confidence interval), and understanding what confidence and significance levels mean.
4. **Hypothesis Testing** — Formalising the question "Is this result real or just random noise?" using null/alternative hypotheses, test statistics, and p-values. Covers both parametric tests (Z-test, t-test) and non-parametric alternatives.
5. **Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)** — Comparing means across more than two groups using one-way and two-way ANOVA, with applications in engineering research and design.
