## Suggested Study Order

1. Start with statistics language and sampling in [Statistics Basics](./statistics-basics.md).
2. Learn how datasets are summarized in [Descriptive Statistics](./descriptive-statistics.md).
3. Move to uncertainty and event logic in [Probability Fundamentals](./probability-fundamentals.md).
4. Finish with modeling random outcomes in [Random Variables and Distributions](./random-variables-and-distributions.md).

## Unit Outcomes

- Identify the difference between descriptive and inferential thinking.
- Classify data correctly and choose a suitable sampling method.
- Compute and interpret core descriptive statistics.
- Solve probability questions involving events, conditions, and rules.
- Use standard distributions to model engineering and analytics problems.
