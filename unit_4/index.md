## Overview

**Functions and Optimization for Machine Learning** connects basic calculus with the algorithms used to train predictive models. Earlier units described data, uncertainty, inference, and linear algebra. This unit focuses on how a model's objective function is defined, how its rate of change is measured, and how that information is used to improve parameters step by step.

The unit progresses through three blocks:

1. **Functions and Calculus** — Understanding linear and nonlinear functions, computing derivatives, applying derivative rules, and interpreting definite and indefinite integrals.
2. **Optimization Foundations** — Formulating optimization problems, distinguishing maxima from minima, and introducing first-order methods that rely on gradients rather than closed-form algebra.
3. **Gradient Methods and Curvature** — Studying gradients and Hessians to understand slope and curvature, then applying Gradient Descent and Stochastic Gradient Descent to machine learning objectives.

## Suggested Study Order

1. Start with function behavior and calculus tools in [Functions and Calculus](./functions-and-calculus.md).
2. Move to objective design and extrema in [Optimization Foundations](./optimization-foundations.md).
3. Finish with curvature and iterative learning algorithms in [Gradient Methods](./gradient-methods.md).

## Unit Outcomes

- Distinguish linear functions from nonlinear functions and interpret their graphs.
- Compute derivatives using core derivative rules and explain what a derivative means geometrically.
- Evaluate basic indefinite and definite integrals and interpret accumulation.
- Formulate optimization problems in terms of objective functions, constraints, and decision variables.
- Classify stationary points as maxima, minima, or saddle points.
- Explain how first-order optimization methods update parameters using gradient information.
- Compute and interpret the Hessian matrix as a measure of local curvature.
- Compare Gradient Descent and Stochastic Gradient Descent in terms of speed, noise, and scalability.
