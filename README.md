# **Analytical Methods in Engineering Systems (AMES)**

the syllabus is divided into **4 units**

### 1. Introduction to Statistics & Probability
- **Statistics Basics:** Types of Data, Types of Statistics, Introduction to Sampling, and Sampling methods.
- **Descriptive Statistics:** Parameters vs Statistics, Point estimate, Measures of central tendency and dispersion, Data Visualization, Correlation, and Covariance.
- **Probability:** Overview of probability and its applications in engineering, sample space, events, probability axioms, Conditional probability, and independence.
- **Distributions:** Introduction to Random variables, Expected values and variance, Common probability distributions (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Normal distributions), Sampling distribution, and Central Limit Theorem.

### 2. Inferential Statistics
- Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing (Parametric and Non Parametric).
- Analysis of variance (ANOVA), including One-way and Two-way ANOVA.
- Applications of statistical methods in engineering research and design.

### 3. Linear Algebra
- Introduction to system of linear equations, Matrices and matrix operations, Determinants and their properties, and Row Reduced Echelon Form.
- Vector spaces and subspaces, Linear independence and basis, Eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Covariance matrices, Principal component analysis (PCA), and Applications of linear algebra in engineering.

### 4. Functions and Optimization for Machine Learning
- Linear and Nonlinear functions, Function Differentiation and Integration, and Rules of Derivatives.
- Introduction to Optimization (Maxima & Minima) and First-order optimization algorithms.
- Hessian Matrix, Gradient Descent, and Stochastic Gradient Descent.
