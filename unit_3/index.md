## Overview

**Linear Algebra** provides the mathematical foundation for representing and solving systems of equations, transforming data, and extracting meaningful structure from high-dimensional datasets. While Units 1–2 focused on statistical reasoning, this unit introduces the _algebraic_ and _geometric_ tools that power modern data science and engineering computations.

The unit progresses through three blocks:

1. **Linear Equations & Matrices** — Representing real-world problems as systems of equations, performing matrix arithmetic, computing determinants, and systematically solving systems using Row Reduced Echelon Form.
2. **Vector Spaces & Eigenanalysis** — Understanding the structure of solution spaces, determining when vectors are independent, and decomposing transformations into eigenvalues and eigenvectors — the backbone of stability analysis, vibration modes, and dimensionality reduction.
3. **Covariance Analysis & PCA** — Connecting linear algebra to statistics through covariance matrices, then using eigendecomposition to perform Principal Component Analysis — the most widely used dimensionality reduction technique in machine learning and engineering.

## Suggested Study Order

1. Start with systems of equations and matrix arithmetic in [Linear Equations & Matrices](./linear-equations-and-matrices.md).
2. Build geometric intuition with vector spaces and eigenanalysis in [Vector Spaces & Eigen](./vector-spaces-and-eigen.md).
3. Connect linear algebra to statistics and ML in [PCA & Applications](./pca-and-applications.md).

## Unit Outcomes

- Represent and solve systems of linear equations using matrix methods.
- Perform matrix arithmetic (addition, multiplication, transpose, inverse) and compute determinants.
- Reduce augmented matrices to RREF and classify solution types.
- Identify vector spaces, subspaces, bases, and dimension.
- Compute eigenvalues and eigenvectors for square matrices.
- Construct and interpret covariance matrices.
- Apply PCA to reduce dimensionality while preserving variance.
- Recognise real-world engineering applications of linear algebra.
